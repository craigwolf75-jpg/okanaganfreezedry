FAQ

Common Questions

Everything you need to know before you book.

```
Is it worth the cost compared to just buying a machine? +
A Harvest Right freeze dryer costs $4,500–$6,000 CAD to buy — and that's before the trailer, accessories, and learning curve. If you're processing one or two elk a year, or a seasonal fruit harvest, renting makes far more financial sense.

The Hunter Package is $699 all-in — machine delivered to your door, sealer, bags, walkthrough, support, pickup included. You'd need to use a purchased machine for 7+ years just to break even versus renting.

Renting also means zero maintenance, zero storage, and zero risk of a $5,000 appliance sitting unused 10 months of the year. For seasonal use, renting is simply the smarter financial decision.
Do I need any experience to use the machine? +
None at all. We do a hands-on walkthrough when you pick up the trailer, and you'll get a step-by-step guide to take home. The machine is fully automatic — load your trays, press start, and it tells you when it's done. We're also a text away during your rental if you have any questions.
How loud is the machine? +
The freeze dryer runs at about 65 decibels — similar to a dishwasher or a quiet conversation. Since the machine stays in the trailer the whole time, just park it in a spot away from your bedroom — a driveway, backyard, or side of the house works great. It runs continuously through each cycle including overnight, so distance from sleeping areas is the main thing to think about.
Can I run it overnight? +
Yes — and you should. Each cycle takes 20–40 hours, so running overnight is the best way to maximize your rental. Load it before bed and wake up to a finished batch. The machine will beep when it's done.
What can I freeze dry? +
Almost anything — fruits, vegetables, meats, fish, full cooked meals, dairy, and eggs. The Okanagan favourites are peaches, cherries, apples, deer, elk, trout, and salmon. Foods high in fat (like pure butter or oil) don't freeze dry well because fat goes rancid over time — lean meats like deer, elk, and moose are ideal. Most whole foods do beautifully.
How do I prepare wild game meat for freeze drying? +
The single most important tip: trim all fat before freeze drying. Fat doesn't freeze dry well and will shorten shelf life significantly — lean cuts are the goal. Here's what works best:

Pro tip — pre-freeze your food before the rental starts. If you bring your meat or produce already frozen and portioned, the machine skips the freezing stage entirely and goes straight to drying. This can save 4–8 hours per batch, meaning you get significantly more batches done in the same rental window. Prep your food, portion it on trays, freeze it flat at home — then load and go the moment the trailer arrives.

Best approach for beginners — pre-cook it. Pre-cooked meat is safer, produces better texture on rehydration, and is much easier to work with. Ground meat, stew chunks, and pre-made camp meals (chili, stew, pasta) all freeze dry exceptionally well and rehydrate back close to fresh with just hot water added.

For raw meat — slice thin (¼ inch or less) for even drying. Thick cuts don't dry evenly and can leave moisture in the centre. Ground meat pressed flat is also excellent raw.

Best meats for freeze drying: Deer, elk, moose, and wild salmon are ideal — all naturally very lean. Avoid anything high in fat like bear meat or heavily marbled beef without trimming first.
How much does meat shrink when freeze dried? +
A lot — and that's the whole point. 100 lbs of fresh meat becomes approximately 25–30 lbs of freeze dried product — roughly a 70–75% reduction in weight and volume. An entire elk (200–400 lbs of meat) can be packed down to fit in a couple of bins that sit on a shelf for 25 years with no freezer required. For hunters, that's the difference between overwhelming freezer overflow and a compact, organized long-term food supply.
Does freeze drying destroy the nutrition? +
Freeze drying preserves nutrition better than almost any other preservation method. Unlike dehydrating (which uses heat and damages protein and vitamins), freeze drying removes moisture at very low temperatures — so protein stays largely intact, vitamins are mostly preserved, and the food retains its original colour, shape, and structure. When you rehydrate, you get something very close to the original. Ground meat and stew dishes come back nearly identical. Whole steak cuts have slightly softer texture but are still very usable.
Does the machine stay in the trailer the whole time? +
Yes. At 140 lbs the machine stays secured in the trailer for the entire rental. Just back the trailer into position, run an extension cord to your 110V outlet, and you're ready to go. No unloading, no moving, no setup headaches.
What if the machine breaks down during my rental? +
If there's a mechanical issue through no fault of yours, we'll repair or replace the machine as fast as possible. You won't be charged for any days the machine is down. Just text us right away and we'll sort it out.
Do I need a vacuum sealer? +
Not a traditional vacuum sealer — standard FoodSaver-style machines don't work well with mylar bags. You need an impulse heat sealer, which uses heat to seal the bag. With oxygen absorbers inside, the absorbers remove the remaining oxygen for you. Add our Sealing Kit ($35) at booking and we'll include a 12" impulse sealer, mylar bags, and oxygen absorbers.
How far in advance should I book? +
During peak season (cherry/peach season July–August, hunting season November–December) we recommend booking 2–3 weeks ahead. Off-season you can often book with a few days notice. Check the availability calendar above and book early to lock in your dates.
```

💡 The Pre-Freeze Trick — Most Important Tip in This Guide

Portion your food on flat trays and freeze it solid at home before your rental starts. The machine will skip its freeze stage (6–9 hours) and go straight to drying. That saves nearly a full day per batch.

Without pre-freezing: 4–5 batches in a week.  With pre-freezing: 6–7 batches. That's the difference between processing 90 lbs and 130+ lbs of elk.

Key Prep Rules

```
✅ Do This
  • ✓Trim ALL fat from meat before loading — fat goes rancid and shortens shelf life
  • ✓Pre-freeze food solid before rental starts — saves 6–9 hrs per batch
  • ✓Slice thin and uniform — ¼" for meat, ¼–½" for fruit & veg
  • ✓Cook starchy veg (potatoes, squash) before loading
  • ✓Score or halve blueberries, grapes, cherries — tough skins trap moisture
  • ✓Let food cool completely before sealing in mylar bags
  • ✓Add oxygen absorbers immediately after sealing
❌ Avoid This
  • ✗Loading fatty cuts — bear, heavily marbled beef without trimming
  • ✗Overfilling trays — leave space for airflow between pieces
  • ✗Mixing very high-moisture food (watermelon) with fast-drying food
  • ✗Opening the machine door mid-cycle
  • ✗Sealing bags while food is still warm — moisture will re-absorb
  • ✗Using a FoodSaver on mylar — use the impulse sealer provided
  • ✗Stacking or piling food — everything must lie flat on trays
```

Times based on 6-tray Harvest Right Large Pro (18–27 lbs fresh per batch). Actual times vary by slice thickness, tray load, room temperature, and humidity. Machine beeps when done — always confirm dryness before sealing.
